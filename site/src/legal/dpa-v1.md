# RoamCode Cloud Data Processing Addendum

Effective: 17 July 2026 · Version 1.0

This Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) supplements the RoamCode Cloud Public Preview Terms when an organization uses the Cloud Service to process personal data subject to data-protection law.

## 1. Roles and instructions

The customer is the controller and RoamCode is the processor for customer-controlled account, organization, host-control, and audit data. RoamCode will process that data only to provide, secure, support, and improve the Cloud Service under the agreement and the customer’s documented product configuration, unless law requires otherwise.

## 2. Processing details

- Subject matter: hosted identity, organization administration, host discovery, authorization, relay coordination, and audit services.
- Duration: the customer’s use of the Cloud Service plus documented deletion and backup-retention periods.
- Data subjects: customer users, invited collaborators, administrators, and people identified in access-request or audit metadata.
- Data types: names, email addresses, authentication identifiers, device and host metadata, roles, grants, security events, audit records, and allowlisted product events.
- Sensitive data: not intentionally required. Customers must not place secrets or special-category data in labels, invitations, or free-text reasons.

Terminal and file payloads are designed to be end-to-end encrypted between authorized customer endpoints and are not intended to be accessible to the control plane.

## 3. Confidentiality and security

RoamCode ensures that personnel authorized to process customer data are bound by confidentiality duties and applies technical and organizational measures including credential hashing, least-privilege controls, encrypted transport, tenant-scoped authorization, rate limiting, log redaction, tamper-evident audit records, backups, vulnerability management, and incident response.

## 4. Subprocessors and transfers

The customer authorizes subprocessors needed for hosting, data storage, email delivery, security, and support. The current list and a change-notice mechanism are published at https://roamcode.ai/legal/subprocessors. RoamCode remains responsible for subprocessor obligations consistent with this DPA. Required international transfers will use an applicable adequacy decision, standard contractual clauses, or another lawful mechanism.

## 5. Assistance

Taking into account the nature of processing, RoamCode will reasonably assist with data-subject requests, security assessments, breach obligations, deletion, and legally required impact assessments. The customer is responsible for responding to requests and configuring access appropriately.

## 6. Incidents

RoamCode will notify affected customers without undue delay after confirming a personal-data breach involving customer data and will provide available information about nature, impact, mitigation, and remediation. Notification is not an admission of fault.

## 7. Return and deletion

On termination, the customer may export available control and audit records before deletion. RoamCode will delete or de-identify customer data after the operational recovery period unless retention is legally required. Backup copies age out under the backup schedule and remain protected until deletion.

## 8. Audit information

RoamCode will provide current security and compliance information reasonably needed to demonstrate these obligations. If that information is insufficient and law requires an audit, the parties will agree on a scoped, confidential review that avoids risk to other tenants.

## 9. Conflict and governing terms

This DPA controls only to the extent it conflicts with the agreement on processing personal data. Applicable statutory transfer terms prevail where required.
